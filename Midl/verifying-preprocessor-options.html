Verifying Preprocessor Options

The MIDL compiler implicitly invokes the preprocessor and does not display its preprocessor switches. In the absence of the MIDL /cpp_opt switch, the preprocessor command line is composed of all the /I, /D and /U switches used in the MIDL command line, as well as /E and /nologo switches. To see the switches passed on to the preprocessor, use the compiler's /confirm switch.

For example, the following line

midl.exe -D_WIN32_WINNT=0x501 -robust -DNTENV=1 -Id:\nt\public\sdk\inc -confirm -Oicf -env win32 -out x86 stub.idl
